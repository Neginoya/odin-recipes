Chicken Adobo

Chicken Adobo is a beloved, classic Filipino dish featuring chicken braised in a savory, tangy sauce of soy sauce, vinegar, and garlic, seasoned with bay leaves and black peppercorns, creating a rich, balanced flavor profile that's salty, sour, and slightly sweet, perfect served with rice. It's known for its tender, fall-off-the-bone meat and a glossy sauce that reduces to a delicious glaze, with variations including drier or stew-like versions

Ingredients

  • boneless skin on chicken thighs
  • soy sauce
  • vinegar
  • brown sugar
  • garlic
  • onion
  • peppercorns
  • bay leaves
  • green onions

Steps

  1. Heat a pan on high heat with oil. Sear chicken thighs skin side down for 1-2 minutes to get a nice color on the skin.
  2. Create marinade by adding soy sauce, vinegar, brownsugar, garlic, onion, peppercorns, and bay leaf to a bowl.
  3. Whisk all ingredients together, and submerge the chicken thighs into the marinade.
  4. Cover bowl with plastic wrap and leave in fridge to marinate for at least 1 hour.
  5. Once marinated for a sufficient amount of time, add marinade and chicken to the same pan used previously with chicken skinless side down.
  6. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low, cover the pan, and leave to simmer for 45 minutes until chicken is fully cooked and marinade has reduced to a thick sauce.
  7. Serve chicken over rice and garnish with thinly sliced green onions.
