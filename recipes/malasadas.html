Malasadas

Malasadas are Hawaii's version of a doughnut. Malasadas do have Portuguese origins and can be considered more of a Portuguese dessert, but have become widely popular in Hawaii which has introduced many to this delicious mix between doughnut and beignet(me included). Unlike a doughnut they have no hole and are not glazed, but are dusted with sugar and can be filled.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cups whole milk
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup and 1 tablespoon sugar divided
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup softened butter
  • 1 teaspoonsalt
  • 4 1/2 cups flour
  • 2 quarts oil for frying
  • granulated sugar for coating
