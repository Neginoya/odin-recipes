Malasadas

Malasadas are Hawaii's version of a doughnut. Malasadas do have Portuguese origins and can be considered more of a Portuguese dessert, but have become widely popular in Hawaii which has introduced many to this delicious mix between doughnut and beignet(me included). Unlike a doughnut they have no hole and are not glazed, but are dusted with sugar and can be filled.

Ingredients

  • 1/4 cups whole milk
  • 1 packet active dry yeast
  • 1/4 cup and 1 tablespoon sugar divided
  • 2 eggs
  • 1/2 cup softened butter
  • 1 teaspoonsalt
  • 4 1/2 cups flour
  • 2 quarts oil for frying
  • granulated sugar for coating

Steps

  1. Heat milk to 110F-115F. Take your yeast an 1 tablespoon of sugar to add to the warmed milk to activate the yeast. Lightly stir and let sit for about 5 minutes until it foams at the top.
  2. Beat the eggs, butter, salt, and 1/4 cup of sugar, mixing with the milk/yeast mixture. Add in 2 1/4 cup of flour to the mixing bowl and mix until combined. Once combined, mix in the rest of your flour until a dough forms. Knead this dough until it pulls away from the sides of the bowl. The dough should be slightly sticky, but you can add small amounts of flour at a time and mix if the dough is too sticky to work with.
  3. Form the dough into a ball and place in a large greased bowl. Cover this bowl with plastic wrap and let rise at room temperature for about 1 hour until the dough doubles in size.
  4. Place your dough onto a well-floured surface and cut into 16 equal pieces. Fold each piece into a ball shape being careful not to work each ball too much. Flatten each ball into a slightly fat disc shape, and place each piece on a small square piece of parchment paper. Cover the pieces and leave to rise for another 45 minutes to 1 hour until they have puffed up.
  5. Shortly before your dough discs have finished rising, heat up your oil in a deep fryer or dutch oven to 350F. On the side set up a place for your malasadas to cool off once finished frying.
  6. When your oil has come to temp, add your malasadas to the oil, only 1-3 at a time. Fry each piece for 1:30 - 2 minutes flipping once the bottom appears golden. Once fully cooked remove and place on your cooling station. Let each malasada cool for a couple minutes.
  7. Once your malasadas are cool enough to handle, roll in sugar until they are well coated and enjoy!
